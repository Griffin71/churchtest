About Our Church

History Summary

St. Francis of Assisi Mamelodi West was established in 1954 as a mission church to serve the growing community in Mamelodi. For over 71 years, our church has been a pillar of faith, hope, and love in our community.

The church began as a small gathering of faithful Anglicans who met in homes before a permanent structure was built. Through the challenging years of apartheid, the church stood as a symbol of resistance and hope, providing spiritual guidance and practical support to the community.

Today, we continue to honor this rich legacy while adapting to meet the needs of our diverse congregation and community in the 21st century.

Mission and Vision

Mission Statement: St. Francis of Assisi Mamelodi West exists to glorify God by making disciples who are rooted in faith, growing in love, and serving with hope in the footsteps of our patron saint.

Vision Statement: To be a vibrant, inclusive Anglican community that transforms lives through worship, fellowship, and outreach, embodying Christ's love in Mamelodi and beyond.

As we walk in the way of St. Francis, we are committed to caring for God's creation, serving the marginalized, promoting peace and reconciliation, and nurturing a deep spiritual life that honors our Anglican heritage while embracing the diversity and dynamism of our community.

Prayer of St. Francis

Lord, make me an instrument of your peace.
Where there is hatred, let me sow love;
Where there is injury, pardon;
Where there is doubt, faith;
Where there is despair, hope;
Where there is darkness, light;
Where there is sadness, joy.

O Divine Master, grant that I may not so much seek
To be consoled as to console,
To be understood as to understand,
To be loved as to love;
For it is in giving that we receive,
It is in pardoning that we are pardoned,
And it is in dying that we are born to eternal life.

Our Ministries

At St. Francis of Assisi, we offer various ministries to serve our community and help parishioners grow in their faith journey. Explore our ministries below and get involved!

Choir Ministry

Our choir leads worship through music, enhancing our liturgical experience with traditional and contemporary Anglican hymns.

Youth

Our youth program focuses on spiritual formation, fellowship, and service for young people aged 13-25.

Community Outreach

We serve our community through food programs, clothing drives, and support for vulnerable populations, living out our call to love our neighbors.

Mother's Union

The Mother's Union supports families and promotes Christian values through prayer, fellowship, and outreach activities.

Women's Fellowship

The Anglican Women's Fellowship provides spiritual growth, fellowship, and service opportunities for the women of our parish.

Bernard Mizeki Men's Guild

Our men's ministry promotes spiritual growth, fellowship, and service among the men of our congregation.

Anglican Men's Guild

The Anglican Men's Guild encourages men to grow in faith and serve the church and community through various activities and outreach.

Lay Ministers

Lay Ministers assist in worship, pastoral care, and the spiritual life of the parish, supporting clergy and congregation alike.

St. Agnes

St. Agnes Guild is dedicated to nurturing young girls in faith, leadership, and service within the church community.

St. Mary Magdelene

This group fosters spiritual growth and fellowship among women, inspired by the example of St. Mary Magdelene.

Women of Charity

Women of Charity are committed to acts of kindness and service, supporting those in need within and beyond our parish.

Sunday School

Sunday School provides a fun and nurturing environment for children to learn about God and grow in faith.
